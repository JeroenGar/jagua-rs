This structure conceptually represents a half-line (which also known as โRayโ).

A ray has a โstart vertexโ r0, that is, r0 is a part of the ray itself, but we cannot make a disk of radius ฮต which contained in the ray around r0 for arbitrary ฮต > 0.

If we consider the vectors from r0 to each point on the ray, then they are all pairwise parallel. Therefore, there exists a โdirection vectorโ v and we can represent each point on ray by the parameterized equation:

r0 + tv (t โฅ 0),

where t is the parameter which is greater than or equal to zero.

We can also think of a ray as the locus of a moving point at a constant velocity from the starting point r0 as time passes. In this case, the location of the point after time t (t โฅ 0) is equal to r0 + tv.
